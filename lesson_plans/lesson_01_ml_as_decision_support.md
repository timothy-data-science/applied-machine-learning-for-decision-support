# Lesson 1: Machine Learning as Decision Support

## Duration
2 hours

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Understand machine learning as a support tool, not a decision-maker
2. Identify where ML fits into real-world workflows
3. Recognise risks of blind automation

---

## Lesson Flow

### 1. Scenario Introduction (20 mins)
Present a scenario:
> A company uses an ML model to approve loans automatically.

Students discuss:
- Who benefits?
- Who is at risk?

---

### 2. Concept Explanation (30 mins)
- What ML does well
- What ML cannot do
- Difference between prediction and decision

---

### 3. Case Comparison Activity (40 mins)
Students compare two systems:
- Fully automated decision
- Human-in-the-loop system

They identify:
- Where judgement is required
- Where accountability lies

---

### 4. Group Discussion (20 mins)
- Should ML replace humans?
- When is automation dangerous?

---

### 5. Reflection (10 mins)
Each student writes:
> “One situation where ML should assist, but not decide.”

---

## Assessment (Formative)
- Group reasoning quality
- Reflection clarity
